Bridging ICT Knowledge and Skills Gap

The Department of Computer Science and Technology conducted a workshop on ‘Computer Networking’ strengthening its long-lasting relationship with the school community, particularly the school students. The target audience, R/Karawita Central College including those of GCE Advanced level students and teachers, was selected with a particular regard to under-resourced schools in Sri Lanka. The workshop, organized by lecturers Mr K Charith and Ms DP Jayathunga, was held at Main Computer Lab on 15 June 2018 amidst a large gathering of lecturers and support staff under the directive of Head, Department of Computer Science, Dr Saranga Premathilake. The workshop, expected to bridge the Information and Communications Technology (ICT) knowledge and skills gap, was acknowledged by the audience.

BevyLabs won Gold at IPITEx 2019

Every year in “Thailand Inventors’ Day”, an international pavilion is set up for exhibiting the potential and interesting invention and innovation of international inventors/innovators. This year 2019 it was known as “2019 Bangkok International Intellectual Property, Invention, Innovation and Technology Exposition” (IPITEx 2019), which was held in Thailand, on their Inventors’ Day during 2 – 6 February 2019. The objective of this event was to improve, develop and create the collaboration between Thai inventors and International inventors. This year, there were 443 inventions from 25 countries at IPITEx 2019.

For this event, Buddhika P. De Silva from Department of csi participated representing a student group called BevyLabs from Uva Wellassa University of Sri Lanka. Comparing the inventions/products of team BevyLabs, Judges were more impressed about the application of “Questions generating platform with machine learning” which is an improved version of Note Review mobile application. (https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.bevylabs.buddhika.notereview&hl=en) At the end of the evaluations Team BevyLabs from Uva Wellassa University of Sri Lanka has been awarded IPITEx Gold Medal and a Special award from Indian Innovators Association at Bangkok International Intellectual Property Invention, Innovation and Technology Exposition on 6th Feb 2019.” BevyLabs Team- Buddhika P. De Silva, Rajith Karunarathne, Isuru Edirisinghe, Praneeth Jayarathne, Randika Madhushan

UWU Robot Battle 2018

“UWU Robot Battle 2018” was successfully concluded for the second consecutive time on December 15, 2018.

The competition was held under two categories; School and Open, where hundreds of young enthusiastic tech guys were took part in the robotics extravaganza.

48 teams were participated under the School category comprising a total of 180 number of school students, whose passion has aligned in the field of Robotics and Automation.

The teams represented districts of Kurunegala, Kegalle, Kandy, Polonnaruwa, Rathnapura, Colombo, Badulla, Ampara and Moneragala. The first place of the School Category competition was won by the Swarnajayanthi Maha Vidyalaya, Kegalle, while the Second Place was secured by the Kegalu Vidyalaya of the Same District.

Kuliyapitiya Central College got the third place out of out of 48 teams of the island. It was astonishing to observe that numerous teams were participated representing certain districts, which considered as educationally unprivileged of the country such as Moneragala, Badulla, Polonnaruwa etc.

Uva Wellassa University is proud to provide a platform for the high capable students coming from the rural areas of the island, where an example can be given from the Ampara/Dehiattakandiya Medagama Secondary School, which presented 05 teams for the competition.

The young students were much eager to take part in the competition and they were also provided with an excellent opportunity to meet and network with the talented individuals presenting the national universities of the country in the open category challenge of the UWU Robot Battle 2018.
